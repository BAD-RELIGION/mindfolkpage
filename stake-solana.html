Staking FAQ

How do I stake SOL with the Mindfolk validator?

Connect a Solana wallet from the staking panel (Phantom, Solflare, or Backpack), enter the amount of SOL, and confirm the transaction in your wallet. Native staking creates and delegates a stake account to the Mindfolk validator; rewards compound automatically each epoch.

Which Solana wallets are supported?

You can connect Phantom, Solflare, or Backpack using the wallet menu on this page.

What is native (direct) staking?

Native staking means you delegate SOL straight to a validator’s vote account through Solana’s stake program. Your SOL sits in one or more stake accounts, rewards accrue each epoch, and there is no separate “receipt” token—your position is on-chain staking until you deactivate. Unstaking starts a cooldown (typically about one to two epochs) before SOL returns to your wallet. This page’s Native staking mode follows that model: you delegate directly to the Mindfolk validator.

What is liquid staking?

Liquid staking protocols pool participants’ SOL, delegate it to validators (often many), and mint a receipt token that represents your share. Mindfolk’s token is mindSOL: you can swap or use mindSOL in DeFi while earning staking-like yield, without waiting through a full native unstaking cooldown to move capital. Trade-offs can include smart-contract and protocol risk, and mindSOL’s market price can drift from SOL (“depeg” risk). On this page, Liquid staking uses Jupiter so you can swap SOL or other supported tokens into mindSOL in-browser, with the receive side fixed to the official mindSOL mint.

What is the Mindfolk validator vote account address?

Vote account: MFLKX9vSfWXa4ZcVVpp4GF64ZbNUiX9EjSqtqNMdFXB. Always verify the validator identity in your wallet before you sign any transaction.

How does unstaking work?

Unstaking deactivates your stake. Funds typically unlock after about one to two epochs before you can withdraw them to your wallet.

Are balances and APY on this page always live on-chain data?

The staking panel may use mock balances and placeholder APY figures during testing. On launch, these values are intended to be powered by on-chain data and real-time validator information.
